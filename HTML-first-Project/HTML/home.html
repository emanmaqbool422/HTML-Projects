Allama Iqbal

Allama Iqbal (1877–1938), known as the Poet of the East, was a visionary poet, philosopher, and thinker from Sialkot. His inspiring poetry in Urdu and Persian awakened the spirit of selfhood, unity, and independence among Muslims.
Through his famous 1930 Allahabad Address, he envisioned a separate homeland for Muslims, which became the foundation of Pakistan. His words continue to inspire hearts and minds around the world.

About Iqbal

Allama Muhammad Iqbal (1877–1938) was a renowned poet, philosopher, and visionary leader from Sialkot. Through his powerful poetry and thought-provoking ideas, he inspired Muslims to seek self-respect, unity, and freedom. His dream of a separate homeland became the foundation of Pakistan’s creation.
